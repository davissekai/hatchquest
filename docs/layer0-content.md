## PREAMBLE

It is a humid Tuesday morning in Madina and you are sitting on a plastic chair in the front room of your family home, laptop balanced on your knees, phone charged to 82 percent, MoMo balance at GHS 10,000. Everyone around you says to wait for a "real job" first — the civil service, a bank, anything with a steady salary. But you have not been planning this for months to back down now. The fan hums, a keke passes outside playing highlife from a cracked speaker, and you open a blank page. It is time to begin.

## SECTOR_OPTIONS

**A: "I am building something digital"** — A platform, an app, a digital service. Your office is your laptop and your edge is speed. The internet is your marketplace.

**B: "I am making or selling something physical"** — A product you create or source. Your challenge is supply chain, quality, and getting it into people's hands.

**C: "I am connecting people to what they need"** — A marketplace, agency, or logistics play. You do not own the product — you own the relationship between supply and demand.

**D: "I am growing and moving something from the land"** — An agri-business, food processing, or rural-to-urban trade. Your currency is patience, seasonality, and access to land.

## QUESTION_1

Here is the thing people always ask: why this, and why now? Tell us about the business you are starting — what it actually does, and what the moment was that made you stop thinking and just start.

*Targets: proactivity vs. reactivity + people-first vs. profit-first values*

---

## QUESTION_2

It is your second week and the supplier you paid a GHS 3,000 deposit to has stopped answering your calls. You go to their shop in Makola and the place is locked. The person next door tells you they have not been seen in four days. What do you do?

*Targets: risk tolerance + autonomous vs. collaborative orientation*

---

## QUESTION_3

A former classmate from university hears about what you are building and wants in. They have savings, some industry connections, and a lot of opinions about how you should run things. They want equal say in every decision. How do you handle them?

*Targets: competitive aggressiveness + autonomous vs. collaborative orientation*
